Dive Simulator

In my final year at Babson College I was apart of the Swim and Dive team specifically the dive team. With one of the last meets of the year approaching I had a goal score I wanted to achieve. I was worried that I would not get that score so I created a simulation using Python in Jupyter Notebook to create 10,000 iterations of the meet to see how well I might do. The thing about diving as a sport is the scores are calculable. Each diver does 11 dives each with a specific (dd) or "diving difficulty."The dd is then followed by a number ex. 2.1, 1.7, 2.2, 3.0 ect. Each of these dd ratingsare also a multiplier. The middle 3 scores from judges are added together and then multiplied by the dd. Usually in small meets there will only be 3 judges so all of their scores count. In larger meets there are five to seven judges where only the middle 3 scores are used to avoid bias in either way. In my simulation I used 9 judges and took the middle 3 scores. First I ask the user to put in their dive code for example a 103c is a forward(1) 1.5(the third number represents a 1/2 rotation-3) tuck (c). I created a dictionary that autamatically fills in the corresponding difficulty. The user is then asked to create a range for each dive, their highest and their lowest possible scores. The simulation runs 10,000 times randomly picking a number between the range for each dive then picking the middle 3 values. So in 10,000 iterations of any possible combination of scores the final likelyhood of what the final score should be is revealed.

Net Worth Recorder

This is Python code done in Jupyter Notebook to record my networth for given stocks at any given point. I am an amateur investor and simply wanted to see if I had sold all of my stocks at their most valuable how much would I have made.

This first section is me describing the stocks I own for python to use yahoo finance to get stock information. I have chosen to omit the dates of when I bought the stock and how much of that stock I bought. I then continue to summarize each stock under the criteria of high and low between two dates I have chosen. That information is taken from the table format and just put into individual rows for each stock describing the high and low. I also describe the current value of the stock for the current day. I multiply the quantity of stock I bought by the highest it has been in a range of dates, the low it has been, and what it is now. In the final section I created a statement that says if the stock is net positive it will tell me it has seen returns and if not it will tell me it has not seen returns and by just how much it has or has not.



In this next section I get the difference in days between when I bought the stock and the current day. I created a for loop where it loops every day starting at the first day I bought the stock then advances one day ending on the current day. Each stock is recorded at its close for the day and then multiplied by the quantity owned for that stock. I add up all the stocks and quantities together to get my total net worth each day. That number is appended to a list which is kept recorded. The 1170 shows the number of days since I purchased each stock and each number is my net worth per day.



This final section is simply the ability to view the previsously recorded numbers.
